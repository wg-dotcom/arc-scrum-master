Our Selection Rationale

Not a Ticket Pusher. A Partner in the Game.

After speaking with Samantha, the Scrum Master model wasn't going to work. Candidates who focused purely on ceremonies and had no clue what was actually happening technically? They couldn't speak to Java services, API integration points, or where the system was strong versus fragile. That wasn't going to cut it.

What started as a Scrum Master search became a Technical Agile Partner. An Engineering Project Manager who can sit with Platform, QA Automation, and Software Delivery teams and actually understand the work. Someone who knows what a Spring Boot microservice does and why an API contract matters. Not someone who just asks "any blockers?" and moves on.

So instead of filtering for ceremony facilitation, we prioritized candidates with real engineering backgrounds. Developers who transitioned to PM. Engineering managers with Agile fluency. Technical project leads who've shipped software in Java-heavy environments. We filtered hard on ARC's stack (Java, Spring Boot, React/TypeScript, PostgreSQL, Docker, Kubernetes, AWS/GCP) and US Eastern hours overlap.

A note on our selection criteria: These are people who understand integration points, system architecture, and how engineering teams actually work. Not just the Agile framework around it. We looked for hands-on experience with Java/Spring Boot ecosystems and real API and infrastructure knowledge. People who can earn trust with developers because they've been in the code themselves.
